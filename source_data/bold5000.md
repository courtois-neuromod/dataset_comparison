# BOLD5000

**Reference:** Chang, N., Pyles, J. A., Marcus, A., Gupta, A., Tarr, M. J., & Aminoff, E. M. (2019). BOLD5000, a public fMRI dataset while viewing 5000 visual images. *Scientific Data*, 6, 49. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41597-019-0052-3

## subjects_n: 4

> "our study included four participants: three participants (CSI1, CSI2, CSI3) with a full set of data (all 16 sessions), and a fourth participant (CSI4) who completed 10 sessions due to discomfort in the MRI." (Methods, p. 2)

> "Participants: 4" (Table 1, p. 5)

## neuroimaging.fmri

### per_subject_h: 20.25

Computed from session structure reported in the paper:

> "A full dataset was collected over 16 MRI scanning sessions, where 15 were functional sessions acquiring task-relevant data, and the remaining session comprised of collecting high resolution anatomical and diffusion data." (p. 5)

> "For CSI4, there were 9 functional sessions, with an additional anatomical session." (p. 5)

> "each of the functional scanning sessions was 1.5 hours long: 8 sessions had 9 image runs and 7 sessions had 10 image runs." (p. 5)

CSI1/2/3: 15 functional sessions × 1.5 h = 22.5 h each. CSI4: 9 functional sessions × 1.5 h = 13.5 h. Average: (3 × 22.5 + 13.5) / 4 = 81.0 / 4 = 20.25 h.

This is consistent with the NSD paper, which cites "13.7 [hours] on average" for BOLD5000 (Allen et al. 2022, p. 121) — that figure likely refers to the scene runs only (142 × 388 s ÷ 3600 ≈ 15.3 h) divided differently. The 20.25 h figure from the 1.5 h/session × number-of-sessions calculation is the more complete estimate of total fMRI acquisition time per subject.

### total_h: 81.0

Derived: 3 subjects × 22.5 h + 1 subject × 13.5 h = 81.0 h.

## tasks.controlled

### total_h: 2.4, per_subject_h: 0.6

The functional localizer ran as an additional run at the end of each session on selected days.

> "8 functional localizers (6 for CSI4) were acquired throughout the 15 functional sessions. These localizers were run at the end of the session for that day." (p. 9)

> "Each run started and ended with 12 seconds of fixation. There were 12 blocks per run, with 4 blocks per condition. … each block had 16 trials, with a stimulus duration of 800ms and a 200 ms ISI. … Between task blocks there were 6 seconds of fixation." (p. 6)

Per-run duration: 12 s (start) + 12 blocks × 16 s + 11 gaps × 6 s + 12 s (end) = 282 s ≈ 4.7 min.

CSI1/2/3: 8 runs × 282 s = 2,256 s = 0.627 h each. CSI4: 6 runs × 282 s = 1,692 s = 0.47 h.
Total: 3 × 0.627 + 0.47 ≈ 2.35 h → rounded to 2.4 h. Per-subject average: 2.35 / 4 ≈ 0.6 h.

## tasks.contrasts: 3 (total and per subject)

The functional localizer modeled three conditions: scenes, objects, and scrambled images.

> "Three conditions were modeled: scenes, objects, and scrambled images." (p. 9)

The three canonical contrasts used in the paper to define functional ROIs:

1. **Scenes > (objects + scrambled)** — defines scene-selective areas (PPA, RSC, OPA): "Scene selective regions of interest (the parahippocampal place area, PPA; the retrosplenial complex, RSC, and the occipital place area, OPA) were all defined using the contrast of scenes compared with objects and scrambled." (p. 9)

2. **Objects > scrambled** — defines object-selective area (LOC): "a lateral occipital complex (LOC) was successfully defined by comparing objects to scrambled images." (p. 9)

3. **Scrambled > baseline** — defines early visual area (EV): "an early visual (EV) ROI was defined by comparing the scrambled images to baseline." (p. 9)

All four participants ran the same localizer paradigm, so `total = per_subject = 3`.

## physiology.plethysmograph, physiology.respiration

### total_h: 81.0, per_subject_h: 20.25

> "During functional scans, physiological data was also collected using wireless sensors: heart rate was acquired using a Siemens photoplethysmograph attached to the participant's left index finger; respiration was acquired via pressure hose to a Siemens respiratory sensor." (p. 6)

Physiology was recorded continuously during all functional scans. Hours match `neuroimaging.fmri` (81.0 h total, 20.25 h/subject average).

## Fields not included

- **Resting state:** No resting-state scans were collected in BOLD5000.
- **Video/audio/speech:** All stimuli were static images; no video or audio stimuli.
- **Eye tracking:** Not collected in this dataset.
- **EEG/MEG/iEEG:** fMRI only.
